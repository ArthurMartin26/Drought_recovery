Population data: Annual gridded population data for Nigeria were obtained from the WorldPop project, covering the period 2000–2020 and downloaded as country‑level GeoTIFF rasters. The unconstrained population count products were selected to preserve continuous spatial variation in population distribution and to avoid introducing administrative boundary artefacts that are not aligned with the project’s grid‑based unit of analysis. Population rasters were inspected for spatial extent, resolution, and coordinate reference system, and aligned to a common WGS84 grid to ensure compatibility with other raster datasets (e.g. rainfall and vegetation). These data will later be aggregated to 10 km × 10 km grid cells and converted to population density measures. Population is treated as a slow‑moving, time‑varying pre‑shock state variable (Xi); for each drought event, population exposure will be characterised using rolling pre‑event averages rather than full‑sample means, to preserve temporal ordering and avoid post‑shock contamination.
